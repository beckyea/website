Foldable, Biodegradable, Bike Helmet

Overview

I worked on this project with Maya Kremien and Philippe Videau during my internship at Autodesk during the summer of 2015. The goal of the internship was to use Autodesk's new Fusion360 CAD software to take a project from idea to design to creation. We decided to innovate a foldable and environmentally friendly bike helmet, to combat the problem many commuters face of carrying a helmet around during the day. After ur goal was to create a snug, eco-friendly, portable helmet. After brainstorming a plethora of implementations, we decided to move forward to prototyping with three of them: nesting rings, a geodesic dome, and the a collapsable shell modeled after an armadillo. We realized that the nesting rings were uncomfortable and prone to breaking and the armadillo shell provided a poor fit, so we moved forward with the geodesic dome. The CAD model shown is sculpted to fit around a model of a head taken from a 3D scanner. The individual triangles fold up and detach into a variety of foldable shapes, including a few that can lie relatively flat. We also worked with Phil from MycoWorks to experiment with Mycelium, a biodegradable alternative to styrofoam which is made of mushrooms. Unfortunately, the timeframe of the project prevented us from fully combining our experimentation with mycelium shaping (since it is a relatively new substance, we were among the first to machine the mushrooms), we made some 3D-printed prototyped, as pictured.
