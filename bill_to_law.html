Machine Learning Predictor for Bill Outcomes

Overview

This was the final project for CIS 519: Introduction to Machine Learning and I worked with Jenny Chen and Graham Mosley. Given an open-ended prompt, we decided to apply and assess a variety of ML approaches in predicting whether or not a bill will become a law. The project was completed in Python and used data made avaliable by US Congress that contained both the text of over 30,000 bills from 2013-2018 as well as metadata about them. We also included additional data about the members of Congress at the time of the bill's proposal

To determine how to best predict the bills, we looked at a variety of features, algorithms, and analysis metrics. (99% of bills proposed don't become laws, so an always false classifier yields a deceptively high result.) This information is included in our write-up included below.
